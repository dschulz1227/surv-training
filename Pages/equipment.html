Digital Cameras

What is a PTZ Camera?
-Pan, Tilt, Zoom
-Cameras able to pan left-right, tilt up-down, and zoom in and out
-Gernally used in wide open spaces or areas that require 180 or 360 degree coverage
What is a fixed camera?
- Cameras that are unable to move.
- Generally in Bullet or Dome styles
- Often in used in areas that a fixed image is always required
- Since they are static, you know it will always cover that area

Analog Cameras

-Standard definition security cameras, AKA CCTV Cameras
-Simple to setup and use
-Coax line with BNC connectors on each end runs directly from camera to either monitor or DVR
-DVR records video and sends to monitor
-Being phased out by IP Cameras

Since our adoption of IP Cameras, most of our analog cameras have been given the capability to transmit data over
twisted-pair wire with the addition of a NVT. That data is sent to a receiver and the receiver uses a Coax cable to
send the image to Encoder that converts the image to digital, giving the swith the ability to recognize the data and
transmit it over the network.

Encoders

A common problem we find within our system is the wiring behind a receiver. Each BNC Port(out) has a termination of Cat wire(in) that correspond to eachother.
When an analog camera shot is down, it is important to check that there is an image coming from the BNC port for that specific camera.
If there isn't make sure the wires are properly terminated and secured within the receiver.

Servers

Avigilon NVR

Network Video Recorder also known as a Server. An NVR is responsible for capturing, saving, and allowing the data to be played back on the Avigilon Control Center Client It also holds the software that makes our digital camerass Analytic-capable. Giving the system and whatever cameras that are connected to that server capabilities for appearance search, facial recognition, license plate recognition and more. The server gives our observers the capability of monitoring live and recorded footage. The server captures and retains image data and retains it until memory is needed, then the oldest data will be kicked off. These servers also have RAID 5 or 6, which is just a fancy way of the harddrives each backing eachother up in case of failure.

Pelco NSM

The Pelco Network Storage Manager is a server just like the Avigilon NVR. It stores video and retains it until space is needed. The Pelco NSM offers up to 250M mb/s throughput, nearly 3 times less than our Avigilon NVR's.
